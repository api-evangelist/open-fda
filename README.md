# openFDA

openFDA is the FDA's open data platform providing free REST APIs for public access to FDA regulatory datasets. Launched on June 2, 2014, and managed by the Office of Health Informatics (OHI) within the Food and Drug Administration, the platform makes high-value FDA public datasets available to developers, researchers, and the public in JSON format via an Elasticsearch-based API infrastructure.

The platform covers drug adverse events (FAERS), drug labeling (SPL), drug and food recall enforcement reports, medical device 510(k) clearances, device adverse event reports, device recalls and classifications, food adverse events (CAERS), tobacco problem reports, tobacco research datasets, historical FDA documents, and substance data.
